The Problem Space

Encryption Is Not Enough.

When you send encrypted data, the padlock is visible. In surveillance-heavy environments, showing that a secret message exists is already a critical vulnerability. And the encryption protecting it? It won't survive a quantum computer.

🔍

Blind Embedding

Traditional steganography embeds data randomly — without analyzing the media. AI-based steganalysis tools detect these statistical anomalies reliably. You're not as hidden as you think.

⚛️

Quantum Threat

RSA and ECC, the most widely deployed encryption standards, will be cracked by quantum computers. NIST has already begun transitioning global standards — the deadline is approaching.

📊

No Capacity Awareness

Embed too much and the carrier file becomes statistically suspicious. No existing tool tells you the safe limit before you embed. It's guesswork that costs invisibility.

🔗

Fragmented Tools

Steganography and post-quantum cryptography have never been unified in a single, intelligent pipeline. You're forced to chain insecure tools together — or build your own.

Our Approach

One System. Two Layers of Invisibility.

PQ-Stego fuses ML-guided steganography with post-quantum cryptography into a single, coherent pipeline. Your data is encrypted before it's hidden — and the hiding itself is guided by AI to avoid detection.

A CNN model analyzes the carrier media to find perceptually safe regions. A regression model predicts exactly how much data can be embedded before detection risk rises. A heatmap shows you precisely where the payload is being distributed.

The payload is encrypted with AES-256, key-wrapped with CRYSTALS-Kyber (NIST-standardized lattice-based KEM), and signed with CRYSTALS-Dilithium — immune to quantum attack.

CRYSTALS-Kyber CRYSTALS-Dilithium AES-256 CNN-Based Analysis DCT-QIM Embedding LSB Audio Stego
L1

Post-Quantum Encryption Layer

Kyber key encapsulation + AES-256 encryption + Dilithium digital signature. Your data is cryptographically locked before hiding begins.

L2

ML-Guided Steganography Layer

CNN models find safe embedding zones. Regression models ensure you never exceed safe capacity. Heatmaps provide full spatial transparency.

L3

Multi-Media Support

Supports image carriers (.pgm) via DCT-QIM in frequency domain, and audio carriers (.wav) via SNR-safe LSB substitution.

L4

Checksum Verification

Every extraction is checksum-verified. Dilithium signatures authenticate the payload origin — ensuring tamper-proof message integrity.

The Pipeline

Seven Steps to Perfect Invisibility.

A structured, automated pipeline from encryption to verified extraction — with smart capacity-driven packetization and ML intelligence at every embedding decision.

01 / 07

Encrypt the Payload

Your message is encrypted with AES-256. The symmetric key is then key-encapsulated using Kyber. A Dilithium signature binds authenticity to every packet.

Kyber KEM · AES-256 · Dilithium DSA
02 / 07

Analyze the Carrier Media

Image and audio files are passed through separate CNN models. The image model performs spatial sensitivity analysis; the audio model profiles spectral frequency characteristics.

CNN · PyTorch · OpenCV · SoundFile
03 / 07

Predict Safe Capacity

A trained regression model estimates the maximum number of bits that can be safely embedded without triggering statistical anomalies or perceptual distortion. Correlation: R = 0.9991.

ML Regression · NumPy · Statistical Analysis
04 / 07

Packetize the Ciphertext

Using the predicted safe capacity as the boundary, the encrypted ciphertext is intelligently divided into fixed-size data packets. Each packet is prefixed with a structured header carrying sequence number, total packet count, and a checksum — enabling ordered, error-detectable reassembly on extraction.

Smart Capacity Division · Packet Headers · Checksum · Sequencing
05 / 07

Generate Embedding Heatmap

A spatial heatmap is generated showing exactly which regions of the carrier are designated for embedding. Green zones: safe. Red zones: perceptually sensitive — avoided. Packets are assigned to safe zones in sequence.

Grad-CAM · Matplotlib · Spatial Mapping
06 / 07

Embed the Packets

Each ciphertext packet is embedded into the carrier across the safe regions identified by the heatmap. For images: DCT-QIM in the frequency domain — robust to compression. For audio: SNR-safe LSB substitution. Packets are distributed in order, respecting the capacity boundary.

DCT-QIM · LSB Stego · Ordered Packet Embedding
07 / 07

Extract, Verify & Decrypt

The receiver extracts packets in sequence, validates each packet's checksum, reassembles the ciphertext in order, then applies their Kyber private key to decapsulate the AES key and verifies the Dilithium signature — fully authenticated and decrypted.

Packet Reassembly · Kyber Decap · Dilithium Verify · Checksum
